Communities upgrade own road

  • By Sabrina Tabi
  • Feb 15, 2023
  • 3 hrs ago
  • 0
  • Facebook

  • WhatsApp

The people of Nanguea and Louvunville villages in East Ambae have celebrated the opening of their upgraded road last September.

According to the chief of Louvunville village, Joseph Garae, the communities had been dealing with the poor road condition for many years.

He stated that they have asked the government for help many times over the years but never got a response.

“This was until the beginning of last year when they made another request to the Public Works Department (PWD) to provide material, specifically stating that the community members are willing to provide free labour, that the PWD responded,” he said.

“Our Members of Parliament (MP) had assisted us in providing rice and meat for our lunch, while the community members did all the work for free.”

The chief relayed that according to the PWD, the road was supposed to take six months to be completed.

However, with the cooperation and man power of the community members, the road was completed within only three months.

The chief expressed that the villagers had really worked had.

“All of us participated, the elderly, fathers, mothers, young girls, young boys, including the children,” he said.

Mr. Garae stated that with the good outcome of their work, they are thinking of extending the road work to other villagers as well this year.

However, as he stated, he “hopes the government- specifically the PWD, will respond if they made another request for materials.”
